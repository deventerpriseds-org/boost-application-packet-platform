Product Specification

Executive Engine

An AI operating system for the executive job search — from inbox alert to signed offer.

v1.0 · July 2026 · Compass Design System · desktop + mobile · working prototype attached

One-line purpose

Turn the flood of job-search alerts landing in an executive's inbox into a managed, AI-assisted pipeline — automatically pulling in matched roles, tailoring every application asset to beat ATS filters, running multi-channel outreach, and coaching through interviews and offers — so a senior leader can run a rigorous search in minutes a day instead of hours.

01Purpose & original intent

The Executive Engine began as a mail-and-calendar organization problem and grew into a full search-management platform.

The originating insight: a senior leader running a confidential job search subscribes to several job platforms (LinkedIn, Indeed, Greenhouse/Lever boards, executive search firms, Wellfound/Otta). Each floods a personal inbox with alert emails. The signal is there — the right roles do arrive — but it is buried, unstructured, and impossible to act on consistently. Meanwhile every application demands bespoke, keyword-optimized materials that take hours to produce by hand, and the outreach + interview + offer choreography is easy to drop.

The original intent was therefore twofold:

  • Organize the intake — route job-alert email into per-role folders, watch those folders, and pull matched opportunities into one reviewable place.
  • Automate the production line — once a role is approved, generate every asset (resume, cover letter, portfolio, intro video, outreach, application answers) tailored to that specific posting, through approval rounds, until it is ready to send.

Nothing should start from a blank page: materials are seeded from the triggering email and a master profile, then refined. Templates always have a sensible default so the user is never blocked.

02The problem

Alert overload
Dozens of alert emails per day across 5+ platforms; the good roles are indistinguishable from noise in a linear inbox.
Manual tailoring
Each application needs ATS-keyword-optimized, role-specific materials — hours of copy-paste-and-tweak per role.
Dropped choreography
Follow-ups, hiring-manager outreach, thank-yous, and debriefs slip through the cracks without a system.
No pipeline view
No single place to see where every opportunity stands, what's hot, and what needs action next.
Interview prep is ad-hoc
Prep, recording, and structured debrief rarely happen; lessons aren't captured or reused.
Weak negotiation posture
Offers are evaluated without benchmarks, leverage tracking, or a prepared counter.

03End goal & vision

A leader opens the app once each morning, spends a few minutes triaging what the engine found overnight, approves a handful of actions, and the machinery does the rest.

The end-state product:

  • Continuously watches connected inboxes, extracts and de-duplicates opportunities, enriches them with company signals and likely stakeholders, and scores fit.
  • Presents a morning briefing leading with the latest inbox-scrub results — how many roles were discovered and for which role families — then a prioritized action list.
  • Lets the user triage by swipe (keep / maybe / dismiss) and, on keep, spins up a tailored application packet.
  • Generates every asset from templates with defaults, optimized against the specific JD's keywords, through approval rounds until ready.
  • Runs multi-channel outreach and a cadence engine, then coaches interview prep → recording → AI debrief, and finally a negotiation tracker.
  • Works on desktop and mobile, in light and dark, adapting to the user's persona (CTO / VP Eng / VP Product).
Success metric

A rigorous, high-volume executive search run in ~10 minutes a day, with materials good enough to clear ATS filters and land higher reply and interview rates than manual effort.

05Core concepts

The opportunity

The atomic unit. Every opportunity carries: company, role, location, comp range, match score, fit tier (Strategic / Good / Stretch), urgency (Hot / Warm / Cool), source + source date, hiring manager, recruiter, a "why surfaced" rationale, and enrichment (company signals, stakeholder map, pain hypotheses).

The pipeline (state machine)

discovered → saved → enriched → applied → outreach → engaged → screen → r1 → panel → final → offer → accepted

Twelve stages. Opportunities advance by user action or automated events. The kanban board and command-center stage-rail both write to this field; dismissed opportunities are soft-removed.

The application packet

Created when an opportunity is kept + approved. Bundles the tailored resume, cover letter, portfolio, and intro video. Each artifact has its own status (todo → drafting → review → changes → approved) and a selected template. The packet has a round counter, a JD-analyzed flag, a covered-keyword set, and a feedback thread. It cannot ship until all four artifacts are approved.

Master profiles & templates

Eight master role baselines seed every tailored asset. Every asset type (resume, cover, portfolio, video, and each outreach channel, and application answers) has a set of templates with an explicit default pre-selected for speed.

06The daily loop

  1. Overnight: the watcher ingests alert emails, extracts + de-dupes + enriches opportunities, writes them as discovered.
  2. Morning briefing (Today): leads with the latest inbox-scrub hero — count of new roles found and a breakdown by role family — then a prioritized "do these next" list, KPIs, this-week calendar, engagement signals, and funnel health.
  3. Triage: the user swipes the queue — dismiss, maybe/research, or keep. Keep → build packet.
  4. Produce: in the packet builder, confirm JD keywords, pick/keep templates, generate + approve each artifact through rounds.
  5. Reach out: compose per-channel outreach; the cadence engine schedules follow-ups.
  6. Convert: prep, record, and debrief interviews; track and counter offers.

07Feature modules

7.1 Intake — inbox monitoring

A three-pane workspace: monitored roles (role families with alert counts) → alerts for the selected family → email preview with the parsed posting and triage actions (push to swipe queue / snooze / dismiss). Backed by an OAuth folder-setup stepper (connect email → create roles → map role→folder → done) so each role family reads from its own inbox folder. Sources: Gmail, Outlook/M365, Yahoo; read-only scopes.

7.2 Today — morning briefing

Opens with the latest-inbox-scrub hero: a large count of roles discovered overnight, a per-role-family breakdown (each with a "N new" pill), source companies, and CTAs (Review in swipe / Inbox monitoring / Re-scan now). Below: KPI strip (active, hot, reply rate, interview rate, days/stage, asset opens), prioritized action list, this-week schedule, engagement signals (deck opens, forwards, ghosting risk), and funnel-health bars.

7.3 Opportunities & Pipeline

Opportunities — a filterable, sortable table (all / hot / strategic / active) with match, stage, and urgency. Pipeline — a twelve-column kanban with drag-to-advance cards and a live stage-funnel visualization.

7.4 Command Center (per opportunity)

Tabbed workspace: overview, contacts, resume, outreach, templates, playbooks, interview prep, activity, analytics. Overview leads with an application-packet band (progress + open) plus Compose / Answers shortcuts, a stakeholder map, company signals, pain hypotheses, tailored-asset grid, status, activity feed, and a compensation band linking to the negotiation tracker. An interactive stage-rail advances the opportunity.

7.5 Application Packet builder

The core production line. A left step-rail (JD analysis → resume → cover → portfolio → video → review) with per-step status. Nothing starts from scratch — the JD step is pre-populated from the triggering email (source, role, comp, location, HM) with keywords auto-matched against the master baseline, so the ATS score opens high and only gaps are flagged. Each artifact step offers a template picker with a default, an editable draft, keyword-coverage meter, version history, and reviewer feedback. Review step gates sending on all-approved; "Request changes" spins a new round; sending moves the opportunity to applied.

7.6 Outreach Composer

Multi-channel drafting: cold email, LinkedIn connect (300-char aware), LinkedIn DM, InMail, cold-call script, follow-up. Per-contact personalization from the stakeholder map, a tone toggle (Direct / Warm / POV-led), a message-template picker with a default (Standard / Value-add POV / Referral intro / Re-engage), character-limit warnings, reply-rate benchmarks, "weave signal into draft," copy, and send/queue.

7.7 Application Answers autofill

Drop a screenshot or file of an application form → the engine detects the questions and drafts an editable, copy-paste-ready block per field (work auth, sponsorship, salary, start date, relocation, "why this company," leadership experience, approach). An answer-style picker with a default (Concise / Detailed / STAR method) reshapes long answers. Copy per-field or copy-all.

7.8 Outreach cadence engine

A queue of scheduled touches with day offsets, per-opportunity cadence timelines, templates-in-rotation with reply rates, and cadence-health metrics (on-track / stalling / recommend recycle).

7.9 Interviews — prep · record · debrief

Prep: likely questions with strength tags and expandable suggested answers, interviewer profiles, a coverage map, and materials. Record: a recording surface with live timer, live AI cues that advance on schedule, live notes, and transcript. Debrief: AI summary with advance-likelihood, per-question scoring, owed follow-ups (draft from playbook), and Q-bank updates; advancing writes the next stage.

7.10 Offer / negotiation tracker

Three columns — their offer, your (editable) counter with live total-comp math, and a walk-away floor — plus a generated counter draft, comp benchmarks, leverage summary, and timing. Accept / counter / decline actions.

7.11 Roles, Playbooks, Assets, Settings

  • Role profiles: eight master baselines; each detail shows narrative, key wins, linked assets, linked playbooks, and comp reference.
  • Playbooks: a tagged library (onboarding, operating, AI/risk, etc.) the engine attaches per opportunity.
  • Assets: analytics on every asset — opens, view time, forwards, most-viewed slides, recent opens.
  • Settings: account, Templates manager (category rail + template cards with primary/variant tags), cadence, connections, notifications, master profiles, privacy, billing, recording consent.

7.12 Mobile

A linked phone experience (iOS-style shell) reached via the Mobile toggle: Today briefing, swipe queue with a real drag gesture (rotation + KEEP/PASS/MAYBE overlays), pipeline, outreach, interview prep, packet list, composer/answers pickers, and opportunity detail. Bottom tab-bar navigation; dark-mode toggle in the header.

09System architecture

Flow: platform subscriptions → Outlook/Gmail folders → the watcher → review → generation → outputs. OpenAI and Google Workspace are shared services called from multiple stages. (Full diagram: Executive Engine Architecture.html.)

  1. Subscriptions: LinkedIn, Indeed, Greenhouse/Lever, executive search firms, Wellfound/Otta — each emailing a dedicated alias.
  2. Mail folders: inbox rules route by sender/subject into per-role folders. Microsoft Graph / Gmail API subscription webhook per folder, auto-renewed; read scopes + send scope for outreach.
  3. The watcher (edge functions): ingest-webhook → extract-opportunity (OpenAI) → dedupe (pgvector) → enrich, with a poll-inbox cron fallback + subscription renewal.
  4. Review: swipe triage drives the opportunities.status state machine.
  5. Generation: a durable generate-packet job fans out — OpenAI produces content values, then Docs/Slides batchUpdate replaceAllText fills copies of master templates, exported to PDF.
  6. Outputs: resume, cover, portfolio, video, outreach (via Graph sendMail), answers, prep, debrief.

Recommended platform services

Edge functions (ingest / extract / enrich / generate / fill-docs / fill-slides / send-outreach) · Postgres + pgvector · durable job queue · cron scheduler (poll-inbox, renew-subs, run-cadence, refresh-signals) · object storage (video, screenshots, PDFs) · auth + token vault (MS / Google / OpenAI OAuth) · vector store (dedupe, match, Q-bank) · observability + OpenAI cost metering · push/email notifications.

Google Workspace generation detail

Master Resume.gdoc holds placeholders ({{name}}, {{summary}}, {{impact_1}}, {{keywords}}…); master Portfolio.gslides holds {{slide_title}}, {{case_study}}, {{metric}}, {{logo_img}}. Fill sequence: drive.files.copy → docs/slides.batchUpdate replaceAllText (+ replaceAllShapesWithImage) → drive.files.export to PDF → link back to the artifact.

11Design system & UX

Built on the Compass Design System — a teal-and-slate SaaS system on Inter, fully tokenized. All surfaces use Compass tokens (--surface-*, --text-*, --surface-brand-*); no invented colors. Violet is reserved exclusively for AI features (the CompassAIBadge).

  • Light & dark: a full dark theme via design-system tokens, toggled from the top bar and mobile header. (Note: dark is driven by a dedicated class to survive the bundle's preview-pin, overriding the same tokens.)
  • Density: comfortable / compact.
  • Accent: brand-token-driven; teal default.
  • Two visual editions maintained in parallel: a hand-drawn wireframe original (for concept/QC) and the Compass "product" edition — same router, data, and interactivity, different skin.

13Tweakable demo dimensions

The prototype exposes live controls that also define real product configuration axes:

  • Persona: CTO / VP Engineering / VP Product — re-filters catalog, baseline, keywords, comp.
  • Demo state: Just started (day 3) / Mid-pipeline (day 24) / Closing offer (day 42) — re-stages the pipeline and metrics.
  • Visual: dark mode, density, accent color.
  • Feature flags: AI features, swipe gestures, interview recording, AI debriefs, cadence engine — each independently toggleable.

14Build phases

  1. Foundation: OAuth email connect, folder mapping, the watcher (ingest → extract → dedupe → enrich), opportunity store, review/swipe, pipeline.
  2. Production line: JD/ATS analysis, template system with defaults, Docs/Slides generation, packet approval rounds.
  3. Outreach: multi-channel composer, cadence engine, send integration, asset analytics.
  4. Convert: interview prep/record/debrief, offer/negotiation tracker.
  5. Polish: mobile parity, dark mode, personas, notifications, partner-seat review.

15Open questions

  • Recording consent + storage policy for interview capture (jurisdiction-dependent).
  • How aggressively to auto-send outreach vs. always require approval.
  • Whether to add literal Compass React components on flagship screens vs. the current token-themed layer.
  • Partner/reviewer permissions model and notification routing.
  • ATS-scoring fidelity — how closely to model real applicant-tracking parsers.
